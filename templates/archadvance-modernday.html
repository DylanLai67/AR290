Archaeological Advancements and Modern Day Techniques

The discovery of Pompeii has changed the way we view the past and ultimately how humans interacted with the environment. This piece of history tells us a story about how ancient civilizations used to interact with the landscape to create a prosperous region. The explosion of Mount Vesuvius was cataclysmic and was well known around the world in light of Pompeiiâ€™s discovery. So well-known that it has given rise to volcanology and advanced the study of archaeology by promoting the study of other volcanoes around the world. When excavating Pompeii there were originally cavities formed by bodies that were buried and preserved in the pumice and ash from the volcano. Originally, body cavities were damaged because it was unknown what the cavities were but eventually, archaeologists have managed to recreate plaster molds by filling the cavities to make body replicas of how the people were buried. This technique was learned and used to help with the rest of the excavation. While this technique may be ideal for volcanic victims there may be other use cases for this technique in the future.

Modern technology for archaeologists has expanded beyond just a shovel and seems to be a science that more than meets the eye. IBM Italy is utilizing computers to comprehensively study Pompeii to map 3D models of the city. The technology allows researchers to display data like building type city distribution. By understanding this distribution we get an more accurate understanding of how and what Pompeiians put emphasis on. IBM is taking technology a step even further and using imaging technology for conservation of the villas. Photogrammetric images are taken at constant intervals and allow specialists to detect changes. Ultraviolet fluorescent images provide info about the original portions of the frescoes to be restored later on and the frescoes age.
