Human Environmental Interactions

The excavation of the Thermapoluim of Regio V is a beautiful piece of history that was frozen in time. The find explains more of the ruins of Pompeii and allows us to understand this ancient time period better. It advances our understanding of human environment interactions by teaching us what kinds of plants and animals the city dealt with. Based off of the recovered artifacts we can see how advanced the civilization was and how technologically advanced they were. Additionally, certain findings like containers for wine filled with beans in order to modify the taste of the wine give us clues as to how the civilization most likely had a strong agricultural presence. This can also be supported with the distinction of whether or not the animal bones found within the establishment are domestic animals or wild. Domestic animals are bred by humans to serve a purpose and may be reflected within the animal’s DNA or morphology. By understanding the types of food served we can see how the people of Pompeii interacted with the landscape. The agricultural scene was most likely prosperous considering that Pompeii has fertile soil and is wedged between two rivers. The environment is a perfect moderate climate with sunny skies and as a result made the Pompeiians financially prosperous (Gonzalez, 2005).
